What to Pack:

Plan for an average high of 58 (14.4 C) and a low of 28 (-2 C) -- this means that you must dress for the cold. Consider bringing a jacket or coat. Gloves, scarves and hats work wonders for keeping you warm. You’ll need to bring appropriate clothing for hiking, mountain biking and Jeeping if you and your teams are planning these activities.

If you are unable to purchase warm clothing ahead of time, you’ll be able to buy some gear from the Tranont Store or from local Park City retailers.

Travel Accommodations:

We recommend bringing your Jeep to the conference if you live within driving distance.

The distance between lodging and the convention center is 15 miles. We highly recommend renting a car if possible. Uber and Lyft will have limited availability in Park City.

Travel Around Park City:

Park City offers free transportation with access to all major resorts and downtown. Other free options between Salt Lake City, Park City, and Kamas are available. Click here for more info: http://www.parkcity.org/about-us/transit-bus

Activities in Park City:

If your team requires services above and beyond the hotel concierge, Annalise Margherita with Destination Services, is here to help with hosted airport transportation, group activities, off-site dining coordination etc. They have years of experience working with corporate groups in Park City and are here to be a resource if you are trying to arrange an activity or dinner for 20 people or more. They have the ability take multiple invoices and present one final bill of services. Generally, exclusive Main Street Dining experiences start around $200 per person and activities begin at $150 per person depending on your team and interests. Please do not hesitate to reach out to her if something like this would interest you and your team.
